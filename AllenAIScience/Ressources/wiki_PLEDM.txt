PLEDM (Phase-state Low Electron (hole)-number Drive Memory) is a type of memory chip, developed in 1999 at Hitachi's laboratory at the University of Cambridge. PLEDM is based on PLEDTR technology.


== Features ==
nonvolatile memory
read/write time less than 10 nanoseconds
one transistor for each memory cell
reduced power consumption
