Glennville (formerly, Glenville and Linn's Valley) is an unincorporated community in Kern County, California.


== GeographyEdit ==
It is located 30 miles (48 km) north-northeast of Bakersfield, at an elevation of 3176 feet (968 m) in the Sierra Nevada foothills.


== HistoryEdit ==
The Linn's Valley post office opened in 1860, changed its name to Glenville in 1872, and closed in 1874. The Glennville post office opened in 1874. The name honors James M. Glenn, blacksmith and early settler, who settled the area in 1857.  Glennville became the trading center for the surrounding valley after the decline of Lavers' Crossing. The original church built in 1866 is the oldest standing church in the county.


=== ClimateEdit ===
Climate type occurs primarily on the periphery of the true deserts in low-latitude semiarid steppe regions.
