Wilhelm Johann Karl Zahn (Rodenberg, Schaumburg, 21 August 1800 - Berlin, 22 August 1871) was a German architect, painter and art critic.


== Biography ==
He became professor in the Academy of Arts, Berlin, 1829. He superintended excavations at Herculaneum and Pompeii; cast the finest bronzes and silver vases for the Museum Borbonico; and was architect of many fine houses and villas in Pompeiian style in England and the United States.


== Works ==
The Most Beautiful Ornaments and the Most Notable Pictures from Pompeii, Herculaneum and Stabiæ (1828–30)
Ornaments of All Classical Periods of Art (1832–39)
