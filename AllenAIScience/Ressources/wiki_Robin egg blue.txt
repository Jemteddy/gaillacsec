The color robin egg blue is displayed at right. It is a web-safe color and an official Crayola color. In the early 1990s, the crayon was originally included unlabeled in Crayola boxes, and purchasers were asked to submit ideas for the color's name. Among the contest winners, this color was named by Christopher Straub (Age: 8).
Robin egg blue, also called eggshell blue, approximates the shade of the eggs laid by the American Robin.
The first recorded use of robin egg blue as a color name in English was in 1873.


== Variations of robin egg blue ==


=== Pale robin egg blue ===
Pale robin egg blue is identical to the following tone of the color robin egg blue displayed on the ISCC-NBS color list: ISCC-NBS Dictionary of Color Names (1955)—Color sample of Robin Egg (color sample #162):.


=== Tiffany blue ===

Tiffany blue is the colloquial name for the light medium tone of robin egg blue associated with Tiffany & Co., the New York City jewelry company.


== Robin egg blue in human culture ==
Architecture
Robin egg blue is a traditional color used for a painted underside (ceiling) of a house's exterior porch roof.
Jewelry
The name "Robin egg blue" has been trademarked by Tiffany & Co.
Literature
In "The Great Gatsby", Gatsby's chauffeur is said to be "in a uniform of
robin’s egg blue".
