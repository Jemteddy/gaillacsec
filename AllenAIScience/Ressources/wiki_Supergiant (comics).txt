== Fictional character biography ==
Supergiant is a member of Thanos' Black Order. She is a mentally unstable omnipath and telepathic parasite who seeks out intellect and devours it.
In the search for Thanos' son Thane during the Infinity storyline, Supergiant and Corvus Glaive laid siege to the Jean Grey School for Higher Learning and easily defeated the X-Men. They left after realizing that Thane was not there.
When the Black Order seized Wakanda, Supergiant was left in control of Black Bolt who she would mentally order to activate the bombs the Illuminati hid in Wakanda's Necropolis. When the Illuminati arrived to save Black Bolt, Supergiant used Black Bolt's ability to defeat the heroes. Upon activating the bomb, Supergiant was faced by Maximus who had the trigger. Maximus triggered the bomb, but also used Lockjaw to transport Supergiant along with the bomb to a distant uninhabited planet where she perished in the explosion of the bomb.


== Powers and abilities ==
Supergiant has telepathic abilities that allow her to mind-control anyone and feed on their intellect. In the animated series, she also seems to possess the ability to enlarge her body and increase in strength.


== In other media ==


=== Television ===
Supergiant appears in the Avengers Assemble episode "New Frontier," voiced by Hynden Walch.
Supergiant appears in the Guardians of the Galaxy episode "Undercover Angle," voiced again by Hynden Walch. She an ex-girlfriend of Star-Lord (who stated that Supergiant broke up with him) and is shown to have size-shifting abilities. The Nova Corps had previously apprehended Supergiant at the time when the Guardians of the Galaxy were infiltrating the Nova Corps HQ posing as Nova Corpsman. When Supergiant was accidentally released, she fought Star-Lord, Drax the Destroyer, Rocket Raccoon, and Groot until she was knocked out by Gamora.

Supergiant appears in Marvel: Avengers Alliance.
