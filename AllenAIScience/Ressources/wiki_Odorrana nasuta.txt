Odorrana nasuta (Hainan bamboo-leaf frog) is a species of frog in the Ranidae family that is endemic to Hainan, China. It occurs near streams in forested regions at elevations of 350–850 m (1,150–2,790 ft) asl. Breeding takes place in streams. It is threatened by habitat loss caused by smallholder farming activities and clear-cutting of forests.


== Description ==
Males measure 57–63 mm (2.2–2.5 in) and females 73–74 mm (2.9–2.9 in) in snout–vent length.
